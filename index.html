Chè Thái

[noun]

Vietnamese Fruit Cocktail

History
Chè is a traditional dessert originating from Viet Nam. The sister of the more famous Chè ba màu, Che Thai is a simple but flavorful explosion of tropical flavors native to Viet Nam. An adaptation of Thailand’s Tub Tim Grob, but incorporating a wider variety of local fruits, jellies, and condensed milk to create a colorful, sweet, fruit-cocktail-style dessert served over crushed ice. The name Thái means Thai, referring to the inspiration for the dish, while the Vietnamese "chè" signifies a sweet soup or dessert. The process is fairly simple, requiring no cooking or preparation.
Purchasing Ingredients
The key to the best Chè is fresh ingredients. If you have access, get the fruits and water chestnuts fresh. If not readily available to you, another convenient option is to purchase the ingredients canned. Any Asian supermarket should carry the necessary ingredients. You can check out any of the following supermarkets: H-Mart, 99 Ranch Market, TNT, Weee! (Online)
Water Chestnuts
Water chestnuts are not readily available or sold loosely in typical American supermarkets. If you are able to find some at your local supermarket, fresh ones will taste better. Most Asian supermarkets should have canned chestnuts available. Most variations use red coloring for the recipe but you are welcome to use whatever color of your choosing.

Ingredients
There are many variations of Chè Thái. You can swap out different tropical fruits and jellies for this recipe. This recipe uses prepared jelly but you are welcome to make your own.
Credit: Savory Sweet Spoon
  • Coconut Base
  • Coconut Milk OR Coconut Cream & Half n Half Milk
  • Sugar OR Condensed Milk
  • Crushed Ice OR Mini Ice Cubes
  • Fruits and Jelly
  • Jackfruit
  • Litchi (Lychee)
  • Longan
  • Palm Seeds
  • Nata De Coco (Coconut Jelly)
  • Grass Jelly OR Pandan Jelly
  • Red Rubies
  • Water Chestnuts
  • Tapioca Starch
  • Red Food Dye

PREP

1.
Open Cans and Drain Juices
If using canned fruits and jelly, drain the juices from the can. You can use a colander for this step if necessary. If using fresh fruits, skip this step.
2.
Cut Fruit and Jelly
Take the grass jelly / pandan jelly, and cut it into 1 inch squares.
Cut the nata de coco into same sizes if not already in cubes.
Cut the jackfruit into long strips about a quarter inch wide.
Cut the Lychee and/or Longan into halves or thirds.

COCONUT BASE

3.
Combine Coconut Milk and Sugar
Take your coconut milk and pour it into a pot. Set the heat to high until the coconut milk is heated, then set the heat to medium. Add sugar and/or condensed milk into the coconut milk.
4.
Bring to a Simmer
Bring your mixture to a simmer. Keep stirring to ensure no condensed milk or sugar sticks to the bottom and is entirely dissolved in the milk. The coconut milk should turn into a slight beige color if using condensed milk.

RED RUBIES

5.
Cut Water Chestnuts
If using fresh water chestnuts, peel the skin off until the inner white portion is entirely exposed. Cut the chestnut into half inch pieces. They should be smaller than a dime.
6.
Add Desired Food Coloring
Place your cut chestnut into a bowl and add your desired coloring, ensuring it covers all pieces entirely. Dry them well before the next steps.
7.
Add Tapioca Starch
Add Tapioca Starch to the bowl. Make sure each piece is thoroughly coated with a thick layer of starch. You can toss it to help evenly coat your rubies. You can use a sifter to remove any excess starch before the next step.
8.
Boil Rubies
In a pot, pour about 2 inches of water. Set water to high and wait for the water to boil. Once boiling, place your coated chestnuts into the water in batches. Stir gently to keep them from sticking. Once they float, you can take them out and place them immediately in ice water.

Assembly

9.
Place into Cup or Bowl
Place all your solid ingredients together in a bowl or cup. Most Chè are served in cups. Top with crushed or shaved ice and pour your sweetened coconut milk sauce over the ice. Serve immediately.
